
ENRON CORP - EXECUTIVE SUCCESSION PLAN
CONFIDENTIAL DRAFT - For Board Review Only
Prepared: October 2000

PAGE 1 - PURPOSE AND SCOPE
This document outlines the executive succession framework for Enron Corporation
as requested by the Board of Directors Compensation Committee.
The plan covers the CEO, COO, CFO, and Division President roles.

PAGE 2 - CEO SUCCESSION
Current CEO: Kenneth Lay (Ken Lay)
Primary successor: Jeffrey Skilling (Jeff Skilling)
Secondary successor: To be determined by Board

Jeffrey Skilling's readiness assessment:
- 8 years at Enron; currently President and COO.
- Led development of Enron Online trading platform.
- Strong relationships with institutional investors and analysts.
- Recommended timeline for transition: Q1 2001 if Lay moves to Chairman-only role.

PAGE 3 - TRANSITION TIMELINE
Proposed milestones:
- December 2000: Board formal review of succession plan.
- January 2001: Announcement of expanded CEO responsibilities for Skilling.
- February 2001: Formal transition announcement to shareholders.
- Target effective date: February 12, 2001.
